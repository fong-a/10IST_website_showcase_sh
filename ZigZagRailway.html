Zig Zag Railway

The history of the Lithgow Zig Zag dates back to the 1860s, when the New South Wales Government Railways began construction of a railway line through the Blue Mountains to connect Sydney with the western parts of the state. The steep terrain of the region posed a major engineering challenge, and engineers devised a series of switchbacks, or zig zags, to allow trains to descend the mountain slopes safely.

The original Lithgow Zig Zag operated for over 80 years, until it was replaced by a new, more direct line in the 1910s. However, in the 1970s, a group of railway enthusiasts began lobbying to restore the historic Zig Zag, and in 1975, the Zig Zag Railway was established as a heritage railway.

Today, the Zig Zag Railway operates a range of heritage locomotives and carriages along the historic Lithgow Zig Zag. The railway offers a range of experiences, from short rides on a vintage steam train to full-day excursions that include stops at historic stations and scenic lookout points.

In addition to its railway operations, the Zig Zag Railway also offers a range of educational and interactive programs, including guided tours, workshops, and special events. These programs are designed to promote an understanding and appreciation of the history of railway transport in the Blue Mountains region, and to inspire future generations of railway enthusiasts.
