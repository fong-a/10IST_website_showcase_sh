The Picnic Train

The Picnic Train is a heritage railway operation located in Maitland, a town in the Hunter Region of New South Wales. The railway offers a range of experiences, from short rides on vintage carriages to full-day excursions that include stops at historic stations and scenic locations. The history of the Picnic Train dates back to the 1980s, when a group of railway enthusiasts established the Hunter Valley Railway Trust. The trust acquired a number of historic locomotives and carriages and began running heritage railway operations in the Hunter Valley region.

In the years that followed, the Picnic Train became a popular attraction, offering visitors a unique way to experience the beauty of the Hunter Valley. The railway operates on a section of track known as the Richmond Vale Railway, which was originally built in the early 20th century to transport coal from the mines in the region.

The operational fleet of the Picnic Train includes a number of historic locomotives and carriages, many of which have been restored to their original condition. The locomotives include the 10-class steam locomotive, built in 1886, and the 59-class steam locomotive, built in 1943. The carriages include a range of vintage carriages, including first-class, second-class, and guards carriages. In addition to its regular heritage railway operations, the Picnic Train also offers a range of special events, including themed train rides, dining experiences, and tours of the locomotive and carriage restoration workshops.

The Picnic Train is operated by the Hunter Valley Railway Trust, a non-profit organization dedicated to preserving the history of railway transport in the Hunter Valley region. The trust is run by a team of dedicated volunteers, who are passionate about preserving the heritage of the railway and sharing it with visitors from around the world.
